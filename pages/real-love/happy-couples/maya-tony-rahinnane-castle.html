Their Story

A Maryland couple who crossed the Atlantic and found their perfect day in Dingle.

Maya and Tony travelled from Maryland to get married on the Dingle Peninsula, and from the moment they arrived, it was clear this was going to be something special. Tony — a pilot — brought his adventurous spirit to every moment of the trip, including the day before the wedding when Ladybird organised a round of golf for Tony and his dad. Tony promptly hit a hole in one. The bar was set.

Her family made the journey from Limerick, his from the US, and together they gathered at the ancient ruin of Rahinnane Castle for a ceremony filled with laughter, emotion, and the wild beauty of Kerry all around them. An easy, fun, and utterly magical day that nobody present will ever forget.

Captured by the talented Shane Turner, who brought warmth and artistry to every frame of their special day.

About the Venue

Rahinnane Castle,
Dingle, Co. Kerry

Rahinnane Castle is a beautifully preserved medieval tower house nestled in the hills just outside Dingle town on the Kerry Peninsula. Roofless and open to the sky, this ancient ruin creates one of the most uniquely intimate and atmospheric settings for a wedding ceremony anywhere in Ireland — raw, romantic, and utterly unlike anything else.

With the Dingle Peninsula rolling out below, the Atlantic glinting in the distance, and the stone walls of the castle enclosing the ceremony like a living piece of history, Rahinnane is a venue that feels genuinely magical. It is the perfect setting for couples who want something deeply personal and unmistakably Irish.
