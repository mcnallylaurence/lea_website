Their Story

A timeless elopement amongst the ancient ruins of Muckross Abbey.

With only a general idea of what they envisioned for their special day, Kelli and Ethan trusted us to bring their Irish elopement to life. They ultimately decided on the breathtaking and historic Muckross Abbey, tucked away in Killarney National Park. Arriving in a charming horse-drawn carriage, they wandered hand-in-hand through the ancient stone archways and surrounding forests.

Their non-religious ceremony, beautifully tailored by their officiant Cara to include readings and their own heartfelt vows, was intimately captured on both digital and Super 8 film. After their vows, they celebrated with high tea at a lovely castle hotel, walked the streets of Killarney receiving well-wishes from locals, and even poured their own pints of Guinness before an amazing dinner.

About the Location

Killarney National Park,
Co. Kerry

Killarney National Park in County Kerry is a breathtaking expanse of rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and ancient woodlands. As Ireland's first national park, it provides a secluded, mystical atmosphere for an intimate elopement, while the nearby Muckross Abbey offers a stunning, historic backdrop for unforgettable photos.

Couples drawn to this region can experience the timeless beauty of the South West, from horse-drawn carriage rides to quiet lakeside vows surrounded by nature. It's an unforgettable setting for a wedding day deeply connected to Ireland's rich history and landscape.
