Who This Affects

  • Any couple or guest marrying in or visiting Northern Ireland
  • Anyone flying home to the US via a UK airport-including London Heathrow, London Gatwick, or Manchester-even if you are just connecting through and not entering the UK as a destination

Important distinction: The Republic of Ireland is a separate country from the UK. If your entire trip stays within the Republic-flying in, getting married, flying home from Dublin or Shannon-you do not need a UK ETA. But if any part of your journey touches UK soil or a UK airport, you do.

What Is the ETA?

The UK Electronic Travel Authorisation is a digital pre-entry screening requirement, similar to the US ESTA system that non-Americans must complete before visiting the United States. It is not a visa. It does not guarantee entry. It is a pre-clearance that must be in place before you board your flight.

As of February 2026: The UK strictly enforces a "no permission, no travel" policy-meaning airlines can and will deny boarding to travelers who do not have an approved ETA on file.

Key Facts

  • Cost: £10 per person (children included-every traveler needs one)
  • Valid for two years from approval, or until your passport expires, whichever comes first
  • Allows multiple entries to the UK during the validity period
  • Electronically linked to the passport you used when applying-if you get a new passport, you must apply for a new ETA
  • Most applications are processed within minutes, but the UK government advises allowing three working days in case your application needs further review

How to Apply

Apply only through the official UK government channels:

  • Download the "UK ETA" app (official UK government app)
  • Or apply at gov.uk/eta
Avoid third-party services that offer to apply on your behalf. They charge significantly more and offer no advantage over applying directly.

Who Does Not Need a UK ETA

  • Anyone with a British or Irish passport
  • Anyone who already holds a UK visa
  • Anyone with UK legal residency

Practical Advice

Apply for your ETA as soon as your flights are booked. There is no reason to wait, and the two-year validity means you can apply well in advance without any risk of it expiring before your wedding. Keep a copy of your approval email on your phone. While the ETA is checked electronically at check-in, some airlines may ask to see confirmation.
