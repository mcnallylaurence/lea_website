Layers, Not Heavy Coats

Ireland's weather does not commit to a single state for an entire day. A sunny morning can become a cool, breezy afternoon and a chilly, damp evening-all before dinner. The way to stay comfortable is layers, not one heavy jacket that is either too hot or not enough.

Pack: light sweaters, a medium-weight layer, t-shirts. Plan to add and remove as the day changes. A combination of layers that you can mix and match will serve you better than anything designed for a single temperature.

Ditch the Umbrella. Bring a Raincoat.

Umbrellas are largely useless in Ireland because the rain often comes sideways. The wind makes a standard umbrella a liability, not a solution. What actually works is a good-quality, lightweight, waterproof rain jacket with a hood. One per person. Pack it in your day bag, not your suitcase.

Sensible Footwear

Ireland is a country of cobblestones, gravel driveways, castle ruins, soft grass, and country lanes. Stiletto heels work beautifully for the indoor dinner portion of the evening. For everything else-the ceremony, the outdoor photos, any sightseeing before or after the wedding-bring stylish boots, comfortable sneakers, or thick block-heel or wedge styles that provide actual support.

Twisted ankles at Irish weddings are more common than you would think. Good shoes prevent them.

Plug Adapters

Bring at least two Type G plug adapters per person. These are for phones and laptops. You can buy them cheaply before you leave-do not assume you will find them easily once you land. And as covered in the electricity lesson: if you are bringing any heat-styling tools, check the voltage before you pack them.
