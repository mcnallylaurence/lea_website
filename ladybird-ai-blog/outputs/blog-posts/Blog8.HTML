Legal Requirements for Americans Eloping in Ireland

This is the part that intimidates most couples, but it's genuinely straightforward. You can legally marry in Ireland as a US citizen.

The essentials: You need to notify the HSE (Ireland's health authority) at least 3 months before your ceremony date. You'll need an apostilled birth certificate and a passport. There's no residency requirement. No waiting period after notification (unlike many US states). The marriage is legal in all 50 states immediately.

Your planner handles all of this. You don't fill out Irish government forms. You don't coordinate with Irish officials. We handle it, and you pay us in USD.

Choosing Your Elopement Location

Ireland has hundreds of potential elopement locations. Here are the main categories:

  • Cliffs and coastline: Cliffs of Moher, Dunquin Pier, Slieve League, Dingle Peninsula. Dramatic, iconic, photogenic. Best for couples who want that "wow" factor.
  • Castle elopements: Dunluce, Rahinnane, Ross Castle, Kinnitty. Medieval romance. Best for couples who've always dreamed of a castle wedding.
  • National parks: Killarney, Connemara, Muckross Abbey. Lush, green, ancient. Best for couples who want natural beauty over dramatic scenery.
  • City elopements: Dublin, Galway. Culture, dining, nightlife. Best for couples who want ceremony plus urban exploration.

Best Time of Year to Elope in Ireland

Peak season (May–September): Green, warm, long daylight. Crowded and more expensive. Windy coastal locations can be unpredictable.

Shoulder season (April–May, September–October): Our favorite. Weather is mild, light is golden for photos, crowds are lighter, prices are lower. Fewer rainy days than you'd expect.

Off-season (November–March): Dramatic stormy light, fewer tourists, cheapest rates. But expect shorter daylight hours and rain. Best for adventurous couples who love moody light.

There's no "bad" time to elope in Ireland. Pick the season that matches your vibe.
