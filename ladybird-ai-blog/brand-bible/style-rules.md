## 1. Content Structure & Length

### Blog Posts
- **Standard length:** 1,200–2,000 words
- **Pillar posts:** 2,000–3,000 words
- **Short posts:** 600–900 words (quick tips, announcements)
- **Structure:**
  - 1 H1 (page title)
  - 2–4 H2 subheadings
  - 3–5 H3s under each H2
  - 150–300 words per section
  - 1 CTA at the bottom (link to /packages, /get-in-touch, or relevant page)

### Page Copy
- **Homepage hero text:** 50–80 words
- **Section intros:** 80–150 words
- **Package descriptions:** 60–100 words
- **Venue pages:** 300–500 words unique content per venue
- **Region pages:** 400–600 words per region

### Email
- **Welcome series:** 150–250 words per email
- **Newsletters:** 200–500 words
- **Transactional:** 100–150 words (confirmations, responses)

## 2. Headings & Typography

### Heading Hierarchy

| Level | Font | Size (Web) | Usage | Notes |
|-------|------|-----------|-------|-------|
| **H1** | Playfair Display 300 | 2.0–2.4rem | Page title only | 1 per page. No italics. |
| **H2** | Playfair Display 300 | 1.5–2.0rem | Section heading | Separates major topics. Can include `<em>` for italics in gold. |
| **H3** | Playfair Display 300 | 1.1–1.4rem | Subsection | Breaks up long H2 sections. |
| **Body** | Alice 300 | 0.95–1rem | Paragraphs | Default font. 1.5 line-height. |
| **Bold** | Alice 600 | Same as body | Emphasis | Use sparingly. Never bold entire sentences. |
| **Italic** | Alice italic | Same as body | Emphasis | Use for quotes, foreign words, subtle emphasis. |

### Style Rules for Headings
- **H1 & H2:** Capitalize normally (Title Case). Do not use all caps.
- **Italics in headings:** Use `<em>` tags in H2s only. Colored in `var(--gold-lt)` #D4AF71.
- **Emoji in headings:** None in H1. Rarely in H2 (only for blog/social context).
- **Question headings:** Use for subheadings sparingly. Keep them genuine questions, not rhetorical tricks.

#### Examples:
✓ "How to Plan an Irish Elopement"
✓ "Irish Ceremony *Traditions* for American Couples"
✓ "What Does It Cost to Get Married in Ireland?"
✗ "GET MARRIED IN IRELAND NOW"
✗ "Why You'll ❤️ Irish Castles (Hint: It's Not Why You Think)"

### Body Text Rules
- **Paragraph length:** 2–4 sentences max. Shorter is better.
- **Sentence length:** 10–20 words average. Vary between short and medium.
- **Line length:** 65–80 characters (web responsive).

## 3. Lists & Formatting

### Bulleted Lists
- **Use for:** Highlights, feature lists, quick tips, inclusions/exclusions
- **Format:** Use `•` bullets (not dashes or asterisks in copy)
- **Capitalization:** Capitalize first word of each bullet point (Title Case)
- **Punctuation:** End with period if bullet is a complete sentence. No period if it's a fragment.
- **Max bullets:** 5–7 per list (more = break into multiple lists)
- **Intro line:** Always precede with context line: "Here's what's included:" or "Things to know:"

#### Example:
✓ "Here's what's included in every package:
• US-based contract in USD
• Unlimited planning calls
• Legal marriage guidance
• Venue curation and booking
- US Based Planner
- Pricing in USD

### Numbered Lists
- **Use for:** Steps, processes, sequences, priorities
- **Format:** 1. 2. 3. (auto-numbered)
- **When to use:** Only when order matters
- **Intro line:** Required. "Here's how the process works:" or "Follow these steps:"

#### Example:
✓ "Here's how to get legally married in Ireland:
1. Register with the Irish registrar (3 months advance)
2. Submit required paperwork (birth certificate, residency proof)
3. Attend your appointment (optional in some cases)
4. Get your marriage license
5. Celebrate"

### Callout Boxes / Highlights
- **Use for:** Tips, warnings, key insights, statistics
- **Format:** Offset with subtle background color (cream or gold accent)
- **Font:** Bold lead-in + regular body copy
- **Example text:**
  - "💡 **Pro tip:**" (informal contexts)
  - "⚠️ **Important:**" (warnings, legal info)
  - "✓ **You should know:**" (insights)

## 4. Links & CTAs

### Internal Links
- **Use descriptive anchor text**, not "click here" or "learn more"
- **Context:** Every blog post should link back to 2–3 core pages (packages, venues, about, etc.)
- **Link color:** Always use brand green `#5B6239` or gold `#B8964E`

#### Examples:
✗ "Read our Packages page here."
✓ "Explore our [four core packages](/packages)."

✗ "Learn more about getting married in Ireland."
✓ "Read our complete [guide to getting married in Ireland](/blog/getting-married-in-ireland-as-an-american)."

### Call-to-Action (CTA) Copy
- **Primary CTA:** "Tell us about your vision" or "Let's talk"
- **Secondary CTAs:** "Explore our packages" / "View venues" / "Read more"
- **Link CTAs:** Use arrow icon (→) in text-based CTAs
- **Button copy:** Action-oriented, short (2–4 words)

#### Button Copy Examples:
✓ "Let's plan this"
✓ "See our packages"
✓ "Read more"
✗ "Click to continue"
✗ "More information available here"

### Email Link Convention
- Every email should have 1 primary CTA button
- Secondaries in body text as links
- Example: "Ready? [View our packages](/packages) →"

## 5. Images & Alt Text

### Image Requirements
- **Format:** JPG or WebP (PNG for icons only)
- **Alt text:** Required on every image. Descriptive, 8–15 words.
- **Captions:** Optional. Use for context or credits.
- **Aspect ratios:**
  - Hero images: 16:9 (1200px wide minimum)
  - Card images: 4:3
  - Thumbnails: 1:1
  - Square gallery: 1:1

### Alt Text Formula
"[What is shown] in [Location/Context] for [Purpose]"

#### Examples:
✓ "Bride and groom at Cliffs of Moher elopement ceremony"
✓ "Stone archway at Dunluce Castle wedding venue in County Antrim"
✓ "Couple laughing during outdoor Irish elopement photography session"
✗ "wedding photo"
✗ "image1"
✗ "Make it stand out"

### Image Sourcing Notes
- **All images must support the brand narrative:** real weddings, real venues, real Ireland
- **Avoid:** Generic stock photos, overly posed couples, overly filtered/saturated images
- **Preference:** Professional photography from Ladybird couples when available

## 6. Special Content Types

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
- **Format:** Question as H3, answer as body copy below
- **Answer length:** 50–150 words typically
- **Include schema markup:** FAQPage JSON-LD (handled in page code)
- **Organization:** Group by topic (Legal, Planning, Pricing, etc.)
- **Link strategy:** Link from answer to relevant pages

#### Example Structure:
```
### What paperwork do I need to get legally married in Ireland?

You'll need your birth certificate, passport, proof of residency in your home country, and (if applicable) divorce decree or death certificate of previous spouse. Ireland recognizes marriages from all US states—you don't need to remarry at home. [We'll guide you through every step](/get-in-touch).
```

### Testimonials / Social Proof
- **Length:** 1–3 sentences
- **Attribution:** Include couple name, year, location
- **Format:** Use quote marks or blockquote styling
- **Authenticity:** Pull directly from real couples; don't paraphrase

#### Example:
"Ladybird made us feel like they had done this a thousand times. Because they had. Best money we spent on our wedding." — *Sarah & Mike, Cliffs of Moher, 2024*

### Before & After / Comparison Content
- **Format:** Side-by-side table or two-column layout
- **Headings:** "Without Ladybird" / "With Ladybird" (or similar)
- **Tone:** Matter-of-fact, not superior. Acknowledge both paths are valid.
- **Length:** 3–5 points per side

## 7. SEO & Metadata

### Title Tags
- **Length:** 50–60 characters (optimal for Google)
- **Formula:** "[Primary Keyword] | Ladybird Ever After" or "[Primary Keyword] for [Modifier] | Ladybird Ever After"
- **Include:** Location (Ireland), action (elopement/wedding), audience (US couples) where relevant
- **Avoid:** Keyword stuffing, clickbait, special characters

#### Examples:
✓ "Elopement in Ireland | All-Inclusive Packages for US Couples | Ladybird Ever After" (60 chars)
✓ "How to Get Married in Ireland as an American | Ladybird Ever After" (60 chars)
✗ "BEST Ireland Elopement | CHEAPEST Wedding Planner | 100% FREE Guidance | Ladybird!!!" (overstuffed)

### Meta Descriptions
- **Length:** 150–160 characters
- **Formula:** Benefit/action + specific detail + keyword
- **Include:** If applicable—a number, location, or unique angle
- **Avoid:** Duplicate descriptions across pages

#### Examples:
✓ "Plan your dream elopement in Ireland with Ladybird Ever After. All-inclusive packages, US-based planner, zero FX risk. See our 4 packages & 30+ venues."
✓ "Get married in Ireland as an American. We guide you through legal requirements, visa info, and ceremony traditions. Complete 2026 guide."

### Internal Linking Strategy
- Blog posts: 2–3 links to core pages minimum
- Core pages: Link to blog posts and other relevant pages
- Venues: Link to region pages, blog posts, packages
- Packages: Link to blog posts, venue examples, get-in-touch

## 9. What NOT to Do

### Copy Errors
- ❌ Use em-dashes (—) in text. Use en-dashes (–) or rephrase.
- ❌ Use "and/or." Choose one or rewrite.
- ❌ Start sentences with numbers without spelling them out ("200 couples" → "Two hundred couples" or "200+ couples").
- ❌ Use special Irish characters (ó, é, etc.) in package names or headings. Stick to plain ASCII (o, e).
- ❌ Avoid "which" in the middle of sentences. Use "that" or break into two sentences.
- ❌ Chain multiple parenthetical clauses. Breaks readability.

### Formatting Errors
- ❌ Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis (use bold or italics)
- ❌ Never right-align body text
- ❌ Never use underline for non-linked text
- ❌ Never mix fonts (Playfair + Alice only; no Georgia, etc.)
- ❌ Never use more than 3 colors in a section (stick to green, gold, cream/warm)

### Tone Errors
- ❌ Never say "magical," "fairytale," "enchanted" (too cheesy)
- ❌ Never pretend to be Irish or use Irish slang ("craic," "top of the mornin'")
- ❌ Never use gendered language for couples
- ❌ Never talk down to the reader or over-explain basics
- ❌ Never force urgency ("Limited spots!" "Book now before it's too late!")

### SEO Errors
- ❌ Never keyword-stuff title tags or copy
- ❌ Never duplicate meta descriptions across pages
- ❌ Never link to competitor pages
- ❌ Never hide text in tiny font or white-on-white
- ❌ Never use noindex on pages you want to rank

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] **Headings:** Only 1 H1 per page. H2/H3 hierarchy is logical.
- [ ] **Tone:** Sounds like a friend, not a corporation.
- [ ] **Links:** At least 2–3 internal links in blog posts. Descriptive anchor text.
- [ ] **Lists:** Preceded by intro line. 5–7 items max.
- [ ] **Alt text:** Every image has descriptive, 8–15 word alt text.
- [ ] **Bold/Italic:** Used sparingly, purposefully.
- [ ] **Color:** Using brand colors only (green, gold, cream, warm, stone).
- [ ] **Fonts:** Playfair Display (headings) and Alice (body only).
- [ ] **Hyphens:** No em-dashes (—). En-dashes (–) or rephrase.
- [ ] **Line length:** No paragraph longer than 4 sentences.
- [ ] **CTA:** Clear, short, action-oriented.
- [ ] **SEO:** Title tag 50–60 chars. Meta description 150–160 chars.
- [ ] **Special characters:** No special Irish characters (ó, é, etc.) in page/package names.

---

## Last Rule

**When in doubt, read it aloud.** If it sounds natural and you'd actually say it to a friend, you're done.
