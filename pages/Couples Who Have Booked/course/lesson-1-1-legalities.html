Important: Is This Lesson For You?

If you are not having a legally binding ceremony in Ireland-meaning you are renewing vows or doing your legal paperwork at home-you can skip this lesson. Everyone else: read every word. The legal requirements for marrying in Ireland are different from anything you have experienced in the US, and getting this wrong has real consequences.

Ireland Has Two Separate Legal Systems

The island of Ireland is governed by two different jurisdictions:

  • The Republic of Ireland: an independent country with its own marriage laws
  • Northern Ireland: part of the United Kingdom, with separate regulations

A marriage license issued in Dublin has no legal standing in Belfast, and vice versa. This is not a technicality-it is the law. The jurisdiction where your ceremony takes place determines everything: which paperwork you file, which office you contact, how far in advance you need to start, and what your certificate looks like afterward.

Make sure you know which jurisdiction your venue is in before you do anything else.

Getting Married in the Republic of Ireland

The most important rule: the three-month notice requirement. Ireland's Civil Registration Act requires that you formally notify the state of your intent to marry at least three months before your wedding date. This is not a guideline or a recommendation. It is a legal requirement. A ceremony performed without it has no legal standing in Ireland.

The three-month clock starts the day your completed paperwork is RECEIVED by the Irish Registrar-not the day you mail it, and not the day you book your venue. Plan accordingly and build in a buffer.

The Notification Process: Step by Step

Call the Irish Civil Registration Service at +353 1 863 8200 and ask for the Marriage Department. Tell them you are residing outside Ireland and want to start a postal notification of intent to marry.

Common misconception: many couples think they need to be physically present in Ireland to begin this process. You do not. The Republic of Ireland offers a postal application process specifically for couples living abroad.

They will send you the forms. Complete them and return them by registered post to Ireland. The three-month period begins when they receive your paperwork-not when you send it. Use registered post so you have a tracking record.

Once received, your notification of marriage is processed. You do not need to be in Ireland at this stage.

This is the part that surprises most couples: you must appear in person at a Civil Registration Office in Ireland at least five days before your ceremony. Both partners must attend. There is no remote or virtual option for this step.

At this appointment you will present your original documents, sign a declaration that there is no legal impediment to your marriage, and receive your Marriage Registration Form-commonly called the MRF, or "the green folder."

Your legal ceremony is performed by a registered Solemniser. Only individuals on the official Register of Solemnisers can legally marry you in Ireland. We handle the coordination of this on your behalf.

After the ceremony, your signed MRF is returned to the Civil Registration Office for official registration.

What Documents Do You Need?

Bring all of the following to your in-person appointment in Ireland:

  • Valid passports for both partners
  • Full long-form birth certificates (these must show both parents' names-short-form certificates are rejected)
  • Proof of address
  • If either partner was previously married: the original divorce decree or death certificate of a former spouse
  • For US citizens: your birth certificate must be apostilled by your state's Secretary of State before you travel. This is not the same as a notarization. It is a specific international authentication process. Give yourself time-the turnaround varies by state.
  • If any documents are not in English, you will need certified translations

The notification fee is €200 per couple and is non-refundable.

The Marriage Registration Form (The Green Folder)

After your in-person appointment, if everything is in order, you will receive your Marriage Registration Form. A few things to know:

  • It is valid for six months from your intended wedding date (not from the date you receive it)
  • You must bring it to your ceremony. No MRF means no legal wedding.
  • If you lose it or need to postpone beyond the six-month window, you will need to restart the notification process. No additional fee is charged for this, but you will need to meet with a Registrar again.
  • If you postpone to a date still within the six-month window, the Civil Registration Office can amend the form.

Who Can Legally Marry You?

Only individuals on the official Register of Solemnisers can perform a legal marriage in Ireland. There are three categories:

Civil Ceremonies

Performed by state-employed Registrars. These are entirely secular-no religious content, no mention of God, no religious readings or music of any kind. Civil ceremonies are typically held at Registry Offices on weekdays, though Registrars can travel to approved venues for an additional fee.

Religious Ceremonies

Performed by traditional religious leaders (Catholic priests, Church of Ireland ministers, rabbis, imams, and others) or by religious celebrants registered through interfaith bodies such as Entheos Ireland or One World Ministries. Traditional religious ceremonies often require pre-marriage courses and at least six months of preparation. Religious celebrants offer more flexibility and can incorporate spiritual elements without formal church affiliation.

Humanist (Secular) Ceremonies

Performed by Humanist celebrants registered through bodies like the Humanist Association of Ireland. These ceremonies have no religious or spiritual content and focus entirely on the couple's personal story. Humanist weddings are now one of the fastest-growing ceremony types in Ireland and are fully legally binding in both the Republic and Northern Ireland.

Where Can Your Ceremony Take Place?

Irish law requires that marriages take place at venues that are open to the public. Specifically, the venue must have a fixed, identifiable address and be theoretically accessible to the public.

Approved Venue Types

  • Registry offices
  • Hotels and registered wedding venues
  • Churches and places of worship
  • Gardens or outdoor spaces attached to registered venues

Not Permitted

  • Private homes
  • Remote beaches without an official address
  • Forests or fields unless they are part of an approved property
  • Anywhere alcohol is actively being served in the ceremony space

If you are planning something non-traditional-an outdoor ceremony, a historic ruin, a scenic coastal location-we confirm approval in advance. Do not assume a location is permitted.

What Are the Required Legal Declarations?

Every Irish wedding must include two specific spoken declarations:

The Impediment Declaration

"I do solemnly and sincerely declare that I know not of any lawful impediment why I, [Name], may not be joined in matrimony to [Name]."

The Vows

"I call upon these persons here present to witness that I, [Name], take you, [Name], to be my lawful wedded spouse."

You also need two witnesses, both aged 18 or older, who will sign your MRF.

Getting Married in Northern Ireland

Northern Ireland's minimum notice period is just 28 days-significantly shorter than the Republic's three months. However, we strongly recommend submitting notice 10 to 12 weeks in advance. The 28-day minimum leaves no room for document issues, immigration checks, or processing delays.

How to Give Notice

Unlike the Republic's centralized system, Northern Ireland processes notices through District Councils. You can submit in person (for example, at Belfast City Hall), by post to your local District Council, or by email for some councils. Each partner completes a separate Marriage Notice Form. The cost is £22 per person (£44 per couple).

Your Northern Ireland Marriage Schedule

After the 28-day notice period passes without objections, you receive a Marriage Schedule. This is your legal authorization to marry in Northern Ireland.

  • It can be collected no earlier than 14 days before your wedding
  • It must be picked up in person
  • Your celebrant must have it in their possession before the ceremony begins
  • It must be returned to the Registrar within three days after the wedding

Immigration Note for Northern Ireland

Northern Ireland applies UK Home Office immigration controls. If either partner is a non-UK or non-Irish national, you may be required to complete an Immigration Status Statement. Non-EEA nationals without settled status may trigger an automatic Home Office referral, which can extend the notice period from 28 days to 70 days. If this applies to you, discuss it with us early so we can plan around it.

After Your Irish Wedding

Your signed paperwork is returned to the Registrar for official registration. You can then order certified marriage certificates:

  • Republic of Ireland: €20 per certificate
  • Northern Ireland: approximately £8 per certificate

Allow four to six weeks for official registration before certificates become available.

Common Mistakes That Delay or Invalidate Irish Weddings

  • Assuming a passport is sufficient (you also need long-form birth certificates)
  • Using short-form birth certificates instead of long-form
  • Not getting US birth certificates apostilled by the correct state authority
  • Thinking the three-month period starts when you book your venue (it starts when Ireland receives your paperwork)
  • Booking your venue before starting the notification process
  • Planning an outdoor ceremony without confirming the venue has an official address and is legally approved
  • Forgetting that Northern Ireland and the Republic are completely separate jurisdictions
  • Not budgeting time for Home Office referrals if marrying in Northern Ireland as a non-UK national
