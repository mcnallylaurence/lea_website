Contracts, Invoicing & VAT

One agreement, kept simple

To keep things straightforward for couples who are often travelling from the US, they sign one master agreement with us. Here is what that means for you.

The Agreement

You enter into a contract with Ladybird Events Limited, our Irish-based company. We sign your agreement on behalf of the couple, so there is no need for you to manage a separate client contract.

VAT on Invoices

Ladybird Events Limited is an Irish registered business. Please include VAT on all invoices if applicable.

Insurance

We require all partners to maintain current Public Liability Insurance. Please ensure your cover is up to date before any booking is confirmed.

Cancellations

If a couple cancels, your agreed-upon cancellation policy stands. We will always communicate any changes to a booking as early as possible.

Payment Timeline

Our preference is to pay a deposit on booking confirmation and then settle the balance in the week before the wedding. That said, everyone runs their business differently, and we do our best to meet your terms. Share your payment schedule with us and we will work together to make it fit.

After Booking is Confirmed

Meeting your couple for the first time

Once your booking is confirmed and your deposit has been paid, we hand things over to you. Here is exactly how that works and what we ask of you in those first few days.

1

You Receive the Day-Of Document

As soon as the booking is confirmed and your deposit is with you, we send you our Day-Of Document. This is a Google Doc Page that lives with the wedding from confirmation all the way through to the day itself. It holds everything we know at that point: the couple's names, their email address, the date, the locations, and any detail that is already locked in. The document is a living one - we update it as the timeline is built out and new information comes in, so it is always the most current version of the day. You will always have access to the latest picture.

2

Add Them to Your Own System

The Day-Of Document gives you everything you need to add the couple to your own CRM or client management system. Their names and email address are yours to use as you would with any couple who had booked you directly. Please treat this information with the same care and discretion you would extend to any client.

3

Send Your First Email - With Us on CC

Your very first email to the couple should include us on CC at info@ladybirdeverafter.com. That is all we need. Reach out exactly as you would if they had come to you directly - introduce yourself, let them know you are so excited to be part of their day, and take it from there in whatever way feels natural to you. There is no script from us. This is your relationship to build.

4

After That, It Is All Yours

Once we have seen that first email land, you do not need to copy us in on anything else. All your ongoing correspondence with the couple - the questionnaires, the planning calls, the style conversations - is entirely between you and them. We do not need to be in the loop on every message. We just want to know you made that first connection.

A note on the Day-Of Document: Because the Google Doc is updated continuously as we plan the day together, it is always worth checking back in the weeks before the wedding. Timeline slots, meeting points, and ceremony details are added progressively. If you ever have a question about something that is not in there yet, just ask - we will either have the answer or we will find it.

Elopements vs. Weddings

Context matters more than a headcount

The difference between an elopement and a wedding is not simply a number. Fifteen family members quietly watching a ceremony is a very different day from fifteen RSVP'd guests with a first dance and a formal structure. We discuss the nature of each booking openly with you before anything is confirmed, so you always know exactly what kind of day you are walking into.

We Are Always Clear Upfront

When we reach out about a booking, we share the full picture: who will be there, what the day involves, and the overall feel. If a day is more complex than a typical elopement, we say so before you agree to anything.

No Surprises on the Day

We put the numbers and the context in writing when we enquire with you. You will always know whether you are covering an intimate ceremony for close family or something that has the structure of a small wedding.

When Scale Changes

If a guest list grows significantly between booking and the wedding day, we will discuss your rates with you accordingly. We value the partnership too much to hide the scale of an event or ask you to absorb unexpected complexity without fair compensation.

Honesty on Both Sides

We ask that you are equally honest with us. If you feel a day has grown beyond what was agreed, raise it. We would far rather have that conversation early than have anyone feel taken advantage of.

Photography Packages & Hours

The right amount of time for every story

Our package structure by time ensures every couple gets exactly what they need. Ladybird sells all-inclusive packages, so photography is built in from the start. The packages below are how we typically frame the time, but we are always happy to talk through what fits the day.

2 HoursEssentials

Covers the ceremony and portraits, or a First Look and the ceremony. Ideal for intimate, focused elopements with one location.

3 HoursStory

Covers a First Look, ceremony, and portraits, or ceremony, portraits, and a second location. The most popular choice for couples who want a little more storytelling.

4 HoursExtended

An extended session designed for more locations or additional storytelling moments throughout the day.

Half or Full DayEvent-Based

These are event-based rather than hourly. For example, Bridal Prep through the Dinner Bell, or Ceremony through the First Dance. Scope is agreed in advance.

Extra Time on the Day

If a couple needs more time on the day, you will never bill them directly. Any additional time is invoiced through us and we bill the couple. Please flag it with us as early as possible so we can handle it correctly. As a rule of thumb, we would never ask that an additional hourly rate exceed your original quoted fee divided by the number of hours booked. We understand that setup, travel, and the nature of a shorter booking all affect your rate structure, and we respect that.

Your Hourly Rate

We find it helpful to understand your hourly rate when we are quoting for extensions or half-day upgrades. We do not ask you to justify it, we simply need to know it so we can quote the couple accurately and make sure you are paid fairly. All payments flow through us.

Travel

We try wherever possible to work with photographers who are local to the area, so that you know the landscape and we do not need to factor in travel costs. If a booking does require travel on your part, please flag this when we send you the initial enquiry so we can factor it into the quote from the start.

On the Day

Your role is to shoot, not to lead

We take the planning off your plate entirely. Every Ladybird day comes with a detailed timeline, a clear meeting point, and a full brief so you know exactly what is expected before you arrive. Your job is to be present and creative, not to manage the logistics.

We Provide the Timeline

Before the wedding day, you will receive a Ladybird timeline that covers where to be, when to be there, and what each part of the day involves. We build this collaboratively in the lead-up and share it with everyone on the team so nothing falls through the cracks.

Transportation Is Not Your Responsibility

We never expect you to transport a couple. Couples either hire professional transportation, drive themselves, or travel with family members. If you ever choose to offer a lift as a personal gesture, that is entirely at your own risk and outside the scope of your agreement with us.

On the Day Coordination

Some of our larger packages include on the day coordination. For couples who opt out of that, we do not expect you to step into that role. You follow the Ladybird timeline and we all work as a team. Photographers naturally become a trusted presence on the day, but leading the couple through the schedule is not something we ask of you unless it has been agreed and compensated as part of your booking.

A Team Effort

We work together in the weeks before the wedding to make sure the timeline is solid and everyone involved knows their part. If something changes or you have a concern about how the day is structured, come to us. We would rather adjust the plan in advance than have anyone improvising on the day.

Artistic Integrity & Delivery

Your work, your intellectual property

We respect your expertise and your ownership of the images you create. Here is how we protect and deliver your work.

Copyright & IP

You retain the copyright and IP of all images. Couples are informed they have a personal-use licence for printing, sharing, and home display only.

No Edits or Filters

Couples are instructed not to edit, filter, or enter your images into competitions without your explicit permission. Your vision is protected.

Sneak Peeks

Most of our couples travel from the US and head back to work shortly after the wedding. We ask for 4 to 5 high-quality sneak peek images within 48 hours so they can share their news on the way home.

Full Gallery Delivery

Final digital albums should be delivered within 6 to 8 weeks, even during peak season. High-resolution digital delivery via platforms like Studio Ninja or PicTime is expected.

Gallery Access

The gallery should remain active and free for the couple to download for at least one year from delivery.

Sharing the Love

Please tag us @ladybirdeverafter when you post images from our days. We will always do the same for you, ensuring your craft is celebrated by our whole community.

Crediting Your Work

We credit your work wherever we possibly can. On Instagram we tag you, on our website we tag you, and we do the same at every touchpoint we have. Occasionally a tag gets missed or a credit does not make it onto a page. It is never intentional. If you spot something that has not been credited correctly, just let us know and we will fix it straight away. Thank you for trusting us with your images.

The What Ifs

Answers for the unexpected

We have thought through the edge cases so you do not have to worry about them. Here is how we handle things if plans change.

Your agreed-upon cancellation policy stands in full. We do not override your terms. If a couple cancels, we will notify you promptly and any deposits or fees already paid to you are governed by the terms in your agreement with us.
We understand that life happens. If you cannot make it due to an emergency, we expect you to lead the search for a trusted, high-quality replacement. If no replacement can be found, a full refund of all monies paid is required so the couple can secure a last-minute alternative. We are happy to assist in the search where we can.
Small fluctuations below 20 guests are absorbed without any change to your rate. If a guest list grows past 20, the event moves into wedding territory and we will adjust your rates to reflect the additional complexity. We will always discuss this with you before confirming anything with the couple.
If you feel a second shooter would improve the day, please discuss this with us before the wedding. Any additional team members should meet the same quality and professionalism standards we hold across our whole team. We are always open to the conversation.
Couples are informed at the time of booking that they do not have permission to enter your images into competitions without your explicit consent. If a couple approaches you directly about this, the decision is entirely yours. We simply ensure they know the rules upfront.
