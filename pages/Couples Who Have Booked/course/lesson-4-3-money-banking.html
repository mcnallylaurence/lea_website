On Your Wedding Day

Let us be direct about this: on your actual wedding day, you do not need to worry about cash, payments, or tips to vendors. Ladybird Ever After handles all vendor payments on your behalf as part of your contract. Your photographers, your celebrant, your florist, your venue-all of it is coordinated and settled through us. You never transfer money internationally, never worry about exchange rates on the day, and never have an awkward moment with a vendor over payment. We take care of it.

For the Rest of Your Trip

Card payments are accepted almost everywhere in Ireland. Visa and Mastercard work without issue. American Express has less coverage, particularly at smaller businesses, so do not rely on it as your only card.

Use a card with no foreign transaction fees. Many US banks and credit card providers charge 1-3% on every international purchase-that adds up quickly over a week in Ireland. Cards from providers like Charles Schwab, Capital One, and many credit unions waive these fees entirely. If you do not already have one, it is worth opening one before your trip.

Always pay in euros, not dollars. When you use your card at an Irish terminal, you may be offered the option to pay in US dollars instead of euros. Decline this. That option-called Dynamic Currency Conversion-uses an exchange rate set by the merchant, which is almost always significantly worse than your card's own rate.

Cash & ATMs

Carry a small amount of cash. Most transactions in Ireland are card-based, but some smaller market stalls, car parks, and rural vendors are cash-only. Withdraw euros from an ATM on arrival rather than exchanging cash at an airport kiosk-ATM rates are consistently better. Use an ATM attached to a bank branch where possible.
