About this region

Wild coastlines, hidden castles and the road less travelled

The north west is Ireland's best-kept secret. While other regions draw the crowds, Donegal, Sligo, and Leitrim remain genuinely wild, with vast stretches of Atlantic coastline, dramatic sea cliffs, and remote islands that few visitors ever reach.

This is Yeats country. The flat-topped mountain of Ben Bulben dominates the Sligo skyline, a backdrop that has inspired poets and painters for centuries. Along the Donegal coast, hidden beaches and ancient promontory forts offer ceremony settings that feel completely private and profoundly Irish.

Couples who choose the north west tend to value authenticity over polish. The landscapes here are raw and unhurried. If you want to feel like Ireland is just yours, this is where to come.

The Landscapes

Three counties, one extraordinary region

County Donegal, County Sligo, and County Leitrim each bring something entirely distinct to a north west Ireland elopement.

County Donegal

Sea cliffs, hidden beaches, and Donegal Castle

Donegal is Ireland's most northerly county and, for many, its most magnificent. The coastline runs for over a thousand kilometres of headlands, sea stacks, and beaches that receive the full force of the Atlantic. Malin Head, the most northerly point on the island, offers a ceremony setting of raw, unforgettable drama. The Slieve League cliffs rise almost 600 metres above the sea -- among the highest sea cliffs in Europe -- delivering a backdrop that no photographer needs to improve. Inland, Donegal town provides a medieval setting with its sixteenth-century castle, while the isolated Gaeltacht villages of the Rosses and Gweedore give couples who want genuine remoteness exactly that. Donegal rewards those willing to seek it out, and the sense of discovery is part of what makes it so special for an elopement.

County Sligo

Ben Bulben, Yeats country, and the shores of Lough Gill

Sligo is saturated in literary and mythological history. The flat-topped summit of Ben Bulben, rising sharply from the surrounding plains, is one of Ireland's most instantly recognisable landforms and has inspired writers from W.B. Yeats to countless others. A ceremony on the slopes below Ben Bulben, with the mountain behind you and the Atlantic glittering to the west, is unlike anything else in Ireland. Lough Gill, immortalised in Yeats's poetry, offers a quieter alternative -- its wooded shores and small islands creating a ceremony setting of genuine stillness. Sligo town adds culture and excellent restaurants, making the county a natural choice for couples who want wildness without complete isolation.

County Leitrim

Hidden lakelands, river valleys, and genuine quiet

Leitrim is Ireland's least-visited county, a fact that makes it one of the most genuinely private places in which to get married. The county is defined by its lakes and rivers, particularly the River Shannon, which rises here before flowing south through the length of Ireland. Lough Allen and the surrounding drumlin landscape have a slow, unhurried beauty that suits couples who want solitude without drama. Carrick-on-Shannon offers charming restaurants and accommodation that exceeds what the county's modest profile might suggest. For couples who actively want to avoid crowds and discover somewhere that feels entirely their own, Leitrim makes a quietly compelling case.

Who It Suits

Is the North West right for your wedding?

Every region in Ireland has its own character. Here is what the North West does best.

Couples who want true solitude

The north west offers genuine remoteness that the more-visited parts of Ireland simply cannot match. Ceremony locations here often have no other visitors in sight, giving you the landscape entirely to yourselves.

Drama and raw landscape

Slieve League cliffs, Ben Bulben, and the Donegal coastline provide some of Ireland's most dramatic natural backdrops. For couples who want photographs that are genuinely breathtaking, the north west delivers.

Authenticity over polish

The north west has not been shaped by the tourist trade in the way some parts of Ireland have. What you find here is the real thing -- unmanicured, unselfconscious, and all the more beautiful for it.

Literary and poetic souls

Sligo's connection to W.B. Yeats runs deep -- the landscape here is the landscape of his poetry, and for couples who value that kind of cultural resonance, there is nowhere in Ireland quite like it.

Couples who prefer the journey

Getting to the north west is part of the experience. The drive along the Wild Atlantic Way through Donegal and Sligo is one of Ireland's most spectacular road journeys, and many couples turn the travel itself into part of their elopement day.

Elopements with just the two of you

The remote character of the north west makes it especially well suited to a true elopement -- just the couple, a celebrant, and a photographer in a landscape that feels like it belongs entirely to you.

Practical Information

Planning your North West wedding

The north west rewards those who plan carefully. Its remoteness is one of its greatest assets, but it does require a little more preparation than more accessible regions. Here is what to know before you start.

Getting to the north west

Dublin Airport is the most common international arrival point, with Sligo around two and a half hours by road and Donegal three to three and a half hours. Knock (Ireland West) Airport in County Mayo serves some UK routes and cuts travel time considerably for couples heading to Sligo or south Donegal. Car hire is essential -- the north west is best explored at your own pace, and public transport options are limited outside of main towns.

Best time of year

The north west's wild landscapes are beautiful year-round, but the weather is more unpredictable than in the south and east of Ireland. May through September offers the best combination of light, settled weather, and accessible roads. The long evenings of June and July are particularly magical in Donegal. Winter elopements are possible and can be extraordinarily atmospheric, but plan for shorter days and Atlantic weather with real intent.

Legal ceremonies in the north west

Every package we offer includes a legally recognised ceremony performed by a licensed Irish celebrant, registered with the HSE. Your marriage certificate is issued by the General Register Office of Ireland and is fully valid across the United States. We handle all notification requirements, paperwork, and advance filings on your behalf, wherever in the north west your ceremony takes place.

Remote location logistics

Some of the north west's most stunning ceremony locations require careful logistical planning -- unmade roads, coastal access points, and Heritage Ireland or OPW site permissions. We have worked extensively across the region and know which locations are accessible, when they are at their best, and what each site requires. We handle all coordination on your behalf so your day runs seamlessly.

Weather contingency planning

Atlantic weather is part of the north west's character, but it is genuinely unpredictable. We plan a weather contingency for every outdoor ceremony we organise in this region -- an alternative sheltered location or a flexible timing window that allows us to work with conditions on the day. Many of our most memorable north west elopements have involved weather that became part of the magic rather than a problem.

Accommodation and post-ceremony plans

The north west has excellent accommodation options from intimate guesthouses overlooking Donegal Bay to country house hotels near Sligo. We recommend booking well in advance for summer dates, particularly in peak season when visitor numbers in Donegal can be surprisingly high. We can suggest and help coordinate accommodation as part of your planning, along with restaurant recommendations for your celebration dinner.

Questions & Answers

Frequently asked questions about eloping in the North West

Everything couples ask us before booking a north west Ireland elopement.

Can we get married at the Slieve League cliffs?

Yes. The Slieve League cliffs are a publicly accessible site managed by Donegal County Council, and with proper coordination we can arrange ceremonies at viewpoints along the cliff path. The drama of nearly 600 metres of cliff dropping into the Atlantic makes this one of the most photographically spectacular ceremony locations in Ireland. We advise on timing and access to make the most of the light and avoid busy visitor periods. Get in touch to discuss availability.

Is the north west accessible for international guests?

Yes, though it requires more travel than some other Irish regions. Dublin Airport is the main international hub, with Sligo around two and a half hours by road. Knock (Ireland West) Airport in County Mayo handles some UK routes and reduces travel time considerably. We always advise couples travelling internationally to allow an extra travel day and build in flexibility around arrival, so the journey itself becomes part of the experience rather than a stress.

What is the weather like in the north west?

Honest answer: the north west is wetter and windier than the south and east of Ireland. It faces the Atlantic directly, and Atlantic weather is changeable by nature. That said, the region has extraordinary beauty in all conditions -- mist on Ben Bulben, dramatic clouds over Donegal Bay, and rain on the cliffs can all produce photographs that no amount of blue sky could match. We always plan a weather contingency. May through September offers the best conditions for predictable outdoor ceremonies.

Is a marriage in Ireland legally recognised in the United States?

Yes. Ireland and the United States mutually recognise marriages performed in each country. Every ceremony we plan is conducted by a legally registered Irish celebrant, and your marriage is registered with the General Register Office of Ireland. We provide you with a certified Irish marriage certificate accepted across the US. See all legal FAQs.

Can we elope at Donegal Castle?

Yes. Donegal Castle is a significant Heritage Ireland site and a stunning setting for an elopement ceremony. We coordinate with the OPW to arrange ceremonies in the castle grounds and can advise on the best positions for photographs and ceremony setup. The castle sits right in the heart of Donegal town, making it easy to combine with a celebration dinner at one of the town's excellent restaurants. Get in touch to discuss Donegal Castle availability.

How many guests can we bring to a north west elopement?

Our packages cover everything from just the two of you up to intimate celebrations with immediate family and close friends. The north west's outdoor locations suit small groups particularly well -- remote headlands and cliff paths are best enjoyed by a handful of people rather than a crowd. For larger groups, the region's country house hotels offer private hire options. We will advise on what works best for your numbers and chosen location. View our packages.
