How Much Does It Really Cost to Elope in Ireland? A 2026 Budget Breakdown

Most couples researching Ireland elopements are surprised by how affordable it can be compared to a US wedding. The average American wedding costs $35,000+. An Ireland elopement? Dramatically less—and you get a vacation out of it.

But "affordable" is relative. Let's break down every cost category so you know exactly what you're budgeting for, and where the hidden fees actually are.

Flights to Ireland From the US

Direct flights exist from Atlanta, Boston, New York, Chicago, and other major hubs to Dublin. Expect roundtrip fares between:

  • Atlanta to Dublin: $450–$650 roundtrip (direct flights)
  • NYC to Dublin: $400–$650 roundtrip (direct flights)
  • Boston to Dublin: $400–$700 roundtrip (direct flights)
  • Chicago to Dublin: $550–$850 roundtrip (direct flights)

These are ballpark figures for standard economy in 2026. Book 4–6 months ahead for best rates. Shoulder season (September–November, March–May) is cheaper than peak summer.

Pro tip: If you're adding guests, the flight cost per person drops significantly on group bookings. A guest adding $400–600 to their own flight cost is essentially paying their own way.

Accommodation Costs

You'll typically stay 3–5 nights near your ceremony location. Here are realistic per-night ranges:

Accommodation Type Per Night Range Best For
B&B / Guesthouse $100–$180 Budget-friendly, authentic Irish experience
4-star hotel $180–$350 Comfort, central location, reliable amenities
Castle hotel $300–$600+ Luxury, full wedding-day immersion
Airbnb cottage $120–$250 Flexibility, full kitchen, countryside vibes

For a 4-night stay, you're looking at $400–$2,400 total depending on your choice. Most couples pick the middle: a nice 4-star hotel at $150–250/night = $600–$1,000 for 4 nights.

Bonus: Many couples add a few extra days to explore. You're already in Ireland—why not spend a week? Extra nights at the same rate.

Legal and Government Fees

Getting legally married in Ireland requires notification to the HSE (Health Service Executive). Here's what that costs:

  • HSE marriage notification fee: Varies by county, typically EUR 80–100 (~$85–110)
  • Apostilled birth certificate: ~$25–50 (if you don't already have one)
  • Passport verification (if required): No fee, but allow time

Your planner handles all of this. These fees are minimal compared to the overall budget.

Optional Add-Ons and Their Costs

Beyond the package and travel, here's what couples commonly add:

  • Videography: $800–$2,000 (half-day or full-day video)
  • Hair and makeup: $200–$400 (typically arranged locally)
  • Celebratory dinner for two: $100–$300 (depends on restaurant choice)
  • Second-day adventure photography: $400–$800 (additional 4-hour photo session)
  • Guest travel coordination (if bringing guests): Included in our planning, but guest flights/accommodation are their responsibility
  • Travel insurance: $150–$300 (highly recommended)

None of these are required. They're what couples add if they want to go bigger.
