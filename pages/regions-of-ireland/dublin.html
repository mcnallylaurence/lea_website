About this region

Ancient castles, coastal cliffs and city romance

Dublin surprises many couples. Beyond the city's Georgian streets and buzzing culture lies a remarkably diverse landscape -- from the quiet castle grounds of Malahide and Howth to the wild headland of Bray and the sculpted gardens of Fingal.

The Dublin coast stretches north and south of the city, offering dramatic cliff walks, hidden coves, and sweeping sea views. It's entirely possible to have an intimate ceremony in a centuries-old castle and be back in the city for dinner the same evening.

Dublin also makes an ideal base for guests travelling from abroad. Direct flights from North America, the UK and Europe mean your family can be there without the logistics of a remote location. Dublin gives you both worlds.

What makes Dublin special

  • Howth Head -- dramatic clifftop walks with sweeping bay views
  • Malahide and Howth Castle estates steeped in centuries of history
  • The Wicklow border bringing mountains close to the city
  • Dublin Airport with direct connections from the US, UK and Europe
  • Combine your ceremony with a city break for out-of-town guests
  • Coastal villages like Dalkey and Skerries for intimate, relaxed settings
  • Accessible year-round with no remote logistics to manage
Who It Suits

Is Dublin right for your elopement?

Every region in Ireland has its own character. Here is what Dublin does best.

Guests travelling from abroad

With direct transatlantic and European flights, Dublin is the easiest arrival point in Ireland. No connection flights, no long drives from the airport.

Couples who want history

Dublin's castle estates and manor houses carry centuries of Irish history. If a venue with real provenance matters to you, Dublin delivers.

Short, time-flexible trips

A Dublin elopement can be planned around a long weekend. City, coast, and countryside are all within 45 minutes of each other.

Couples wanting a city celebration

Elope in the morning, celebrate over dinner in one of Dublin's acclaimed restaurants that same evening. The city is your reception venue.

Coastal ceremony settings

From the Howth cliffs to the beaches of North County Dublin, the coastline offers dramatic backdrops without travelling to the Wild Atlantic Way.

Year-round weddings

Unlike more remote regions, Dublin's mild, sheltered climate and covered venue options make it a reliable choice in any season.
