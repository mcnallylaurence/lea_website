Why Travel Insurance Matters

We strongly recommend that every couple-and every guest-purchases travel insurance before an international trip. This is not a box-ticking exercise. It is the difference between a bad situation and a financially ruinous one.

Think about what is at stake: transatlantic flights, accommodation across multiple nights, vendor deposits, and a wedding that cannot simply be rescheduled at short notice. A missed connection, a medical emergency, a lost suitcase containing the dress, or an unexpected illness the week before departure can unravel plans that took a year to build.

Travel insurance covers the financial exposure when things go wrong.

What Good Travel Insurance Should Cover

  • Trip cancellation and interruption
  • Emergency medical treatment abroad (US insurance rarely covers international care)
  • Medical evacuation
  • Baggage loss or delay
  • Flight delay and missed connection

When to Buy Your Policy

Buy your policy as soon as you book your flights. Most cancellation coverage only applies to events that occur after the policy is purchased-not before. Waiting until the week before you travel leaves the majority of your financial exposure unprotected.
