Trains and Buses (Better Than You Expect)

If you are not renting a car, Ireland's public transport network is genuinely impressive - clean, comfortable, and reliable between major cities.

Irish Rail (Iarnród Éireann)

Irish Rail connects Dublin to all major cities: Galway, Cork, Belfast, and Kerry. The trains have free Wi-Fi, a dining car, and scenic views of the countryside that are worth the journey on their own. Book tickets online in advance to guarantee a seat, especially during peak summer months.

Luxury Coach Services

Leave any preconceptions about bus travel at the door. Ireland's intercity coach network is excellent. Companies like Bus Éireann, Citylink, and Dublin Coach run direct express routes from the airports to major towns, with plush reclining seats, onboard restrooms, charging ports, and free Wi-Fi. They are a comfortable and affordable option for guests who are not renting a car.

Important limitation: trains and buses serve cities and towns, not rural countryside venues. If your wedding venue is outside a major town-which many of our favorites are-guests will still need a car or taxi for the final leg.

Renting a Car: What You Need to Know Before You Book

Renting a car is the best way to explore Ireland at your own pace and reach venues that public transport simply does not serve. But there are several things the rental desk will not volunteer upfront.

Driving is on the Left

Traffic moves on the left side of the road in Ireland. Most people adjust within about 20 minutes, but it requires full attention, especially at roundabouts and on narrow country lanes. We strongly recommend renting an automatic transmission vehicle unless you are very confident driving a manual with your left hand. Many US drivers are not accustomed to shifting with their non-dominant hand while also navigating unfamiliar roads-do not make the trip harder than it needs to be.

The Cross-Border Fee

If you plan to drive from the Republic of Ireland into Northern Ireland-for example, to visit the Giant's Causeway or a venue in County Antrim-you must tell the rental desk before you drive off the lot. Most companies charge an additional cross-border insurance fee. Failing to declare this upfront can void your insurance coverage entirely.

The One-Way Drop-Off Charge

Picking up a car in Dublin and dropping it off at Shannon or Cork can add anywhere from €100 to €500 or more to your bill, depending on the company and time of year. If your travel plans involve flying into one airport and out of another, factor this in when comparing rental prices. Sometimes it is cheaper to drive back to your original airport than to pay the drop-off fee.

Book Early

Rental cars in Ireland sell out during the summer months. Book yours the moment you have your flights confirmed. Waiting until a few weeks before your trip significantly limits your options and drives up the price.

Private Drivers and Group Transport

If driving on the left sounds like too much stress - or if you simply want to enjoy the journey without navigating narrow hedgerow-lined roads - hiring a private driver or booking a mini-coach for your group is an excellent option. You get a knowledgeable local behind the wheel, door-to-door service, and the freedom to relax and take in the scenery. For wedding groups, this option also means no one has to worry about parking, directions, or staying sober.

Taxis, Uber, and the Rural Reality

This is the most important transport advice we can give you: do not rely on ride-sharing apps outside of major cities and towns.

How Uber Works in Ireland

Uber operates in Ireland, but it works differently than in the US. Irish law requires that only licensed taxi drivers can use the Uber platform. You are not getting a private citizen in their personal car-you are getting a standard licensed taxi that happens to be dispatched through the app.

The App to Download Instead

Skip Lyft-it does not operate in Ireland. Download FREENOW. It is the primary taxi-hailing app used across the island and works well in cities and larger towns.

The Rural Reality: Pre-Booking is Non-Negotiable

Apps like Uber and FREENOW only function where there are taxis actively working-which means cities and larger towns. If your venue, your Airbnb, or your B&B is in the rural Irish countryside, there are no cabs circling nearby and no app will find you one.

Local taxi drivers in rural Ireland are typically small independent operators. If you need a car at 11:00 PM or 2:00 AM after a wedding celebration, that ride must be booked days-sometimes weeks-in advance by phone or WhatsApp.

We will provide you with local taxi contact numbers closer to the wedding date. Please use them proactively. Do not assume you will be able to arrange a ride on the night itself.
