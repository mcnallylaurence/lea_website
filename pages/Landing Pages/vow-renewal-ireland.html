Everything you need to know about renewing vows in Ireland

Planning a vow renewal from the US to Ireland raises a lot of questions. Here are the ones we hear most from American couples.

No. This is one of the most important things to understand about a vow renewal - it is purely ceremonial. Unlike a legal wedding, there are no notices to file, no registrar appointments, no required documents, and no waiting periods. You are simply reaffirming your love in a meaningful ceremony. This makes a vow renewal in Ireland extraordinarily flexible and straightforward to plan.
We recommend booking 3-12 months in advance. Because there are no legal notice periods for vow renewals, you have more flexibility than a legal wedding. However, premium venues, our top photographers, and the most beautiful months (May through September) do book out. For milestone anniversaries like a 25th or 50th, we recommend planning 6-12 months out to secure your ideal date and location.
May through September offers the best weather and the longest daylight hours - golden hour in June doesn't begin until after 9pm, creating extraordinary light for photography. That said, Ireland is genuinely beautiful year-round. Autumn and winter ceremonies have their own magic - stormy Atlantic drama, mist rolling over the hills, and a sense of intimacy that summer cannot replicate. We help you find the perfect time for your vision.
We work with couples celebrating all kinds of milestones - 10th, 25th, 30th, and 50th anniversaries are the most common, but we have also planned renewals for couples celebrating a new chapter after a difficult period, or simply because Ireland was always "the dream." There is no wrong reason to renew your vows - any anniversary that feels significant to you is the right one.
