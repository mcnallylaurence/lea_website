### Full Content Outline

**Section 1: Introduction (150-200 words)**
Open with the frustration couples feel when every vendor says "all inclusive" but means something different. Acknowledge that when you are planning from the US, you need clarity on what is covered before you commit. Mention that Ladybird Ever After has planned hundreds of Ireland elopements and you see the same confusion constantly. Set up the post as a no-nonsense breakdown.

**Section 2: What "All-Inclusive" Typically Means for Ireland Elopements (300-400 words)**
H2: What Does All-Inclusive Actually Mean for an Ireland Elopement?

Walk through the core components that a genuinely all-inclusive package should cover:
- Legal ceremony (officiant, legal paperwork, notification to the HSE)
- Venue or ceremony location (whether it is a castle, cliffs, national park, or private estate)
- Permit and access fees for protected sites (Cliffs of Moher, national parks, castle grounds)
- Professional photography (specify hours, not just "included")
- Planning and coordination from a dedicated planner
- Accommodation recommendations or booking assistance

Emphasize that "all inclusive" in Ireland is NOT the same as an all-inclusive resort. There are no wristbands. The term means your ceremony day is handled end to end.

**Section 3: What's Usually NOT Included (Even in "All-Inclusive" Packages) (300-400 words)**
H2: What's Usually NOT Included -- Even When They Say All-Inclusive

Be direct and helpful. Cover:
- Flights to Ireland (no package covers this)
- Your wedding attire
- Guest travel and accommodations
- Hair and makeup (some packages include this, many do not)
- Videography (usually an add-on)
- Celebratory dinner or reception (depends entirely on the package tier)
- Marriage license fees in Ireland (the HSE notification fee)
- Travel insurance

This section builds trust by being honest about what couples should budget for beyond the package price.

**Section 4: The Difference Between "Elopement Package" and "All-Inclusive Elopement Package" (200-300 words)**
H2: Elopement Package vs. All-Inclusive: What's the Difference?

Explain that a basic elopement package might only include an officiant and a location, while an all-inclusive package wraps in the planning, photography, legal work, and logistics. Use a simple comparison (not a table with your specific pricing -- keep it general). The point is to help couples know what questions to ask any vendor.

**Section 5: What to Look for When Comparing Packages From the US (300-400 words)**
H2: How to Compare Ireland Elopement Packages When You're Planning From the US

This is your differentiator section. Cover:
- Is the planner US-based or Ireland-based? (Timezone, communication style, USD pricing)
- Do they handle the legal notification process or just point you to the HSE website?
- Are permits for protected sites included or extra?
- Is the photographer their team or a third-party referral?
- What happens if weather forces a location change?
- Do they have backup plans built into the package?

Mention (without being salesy) that Ladybird Ever After is US-based in Atlanta, prices in USD, and handles the legal paperwork and permits as part of every package.

**Section 6: Popular All-Inclusive Elopement Styles in Ireland (300-400 words)**
H2: Popular All-Inclusive Elopement Styles

Brief descriptions of the main "flavors" couples choose:
- Cliffs and coastline (Cliffs of Moher, Dunquin Pier, Dingle Peninsula)
- Castle elopements (Dunluce, Rahinnane, Ross Castle, Kinnitty)
- National park elopements (Killarney, with Muckross Abbey)
- City elopements (Dublin, with options for a pub celebration after)
- Multi-location adventure days (ceremony at one spot, portraits at another, pub dinner to finish)

Link each to the relevant venue page or location page on the site.
