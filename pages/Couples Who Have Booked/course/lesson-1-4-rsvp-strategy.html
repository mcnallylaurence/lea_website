Why Your Guest Count Is the Single Most Important Number

Before we look at a single venue, we need a realistic estimate of your guest list. In Ireland, venues are strict about numbers in both directions.

Minimums: Many venues require a guaranteed minimum. If you commit to 80 guests and 50 show up, you are still paying for 80. This is standard practice at Irish hotels and estates and is non-negotiable once contracts are signed.

Maximums: Historic buildings, castles, and restaurants have hard capacity limits. If a dining room holds 60, we cannot legally or safely seat 65.

The danger of scope creep: Once we book your venue around a specific guest count, adding large numbers of guests later creates serious problems.

  • If you tell us you are having 20 guests and we book a private dining room at a top restaurant, then you want to invite 50 people, that restaurant cannot accommodate you. We would have to find an entirely different venue from scratch-which is extremely difficult to do on a compressed timeline.
  • Scaling down is logistically easier, but you will end up in a space that feels too large for your group, which changes the atmosphere of the day.
Be honest with yourselves from the very beginning about how many people you genuinely intend to invite. It is the number that drives every other decision.

Your Destination RSVP Timeline

12 months out: Send your save-the-dates and open the RSVP process. Your guests need this much lead time to plan and book international travel. The earlier they can lock in flights, the better the prices they can find-which increases the likelihood that they will actually commit.

2 months out: This is your hard RSVP cutoff. By this point, anyone who is genuinely coming to Ireland has made their decision. This number goes directly into your venue contract, catering quote, and final logistics plan. We need it to be accurate.

If you have a very small guest list-parents, a handful of close friends, and people you talk to regularly-the process is more informal. But if you are inviting people who may be on the fence about international travel, a structured RSVP process with a firm deadline protects everyone.
