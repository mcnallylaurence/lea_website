Your Questions Answered

Everything you need to know about getting married in Ireland

Planning a destination wedding from the US raises a lot of questions. Here are the ones we hear most from American couples.

To legally marry in Ireland as a US couple, you must give at least 3 months' notice of intention to marry to a Civil Registrar in Ireland. You'll need valid passports and original birth certificates, plus divorce decrees if previously married. Your marriage is fully recognized back in the US. We guide you through every step of this process - it's included in every package.
We recommend starting planning 12-18 months out for destination weddings. The 3-month legal notice period sets the absolute minimum, but for larger weddings with specific venue requirements, guest accommodation, and peak season dates, earlier is much better. Popular venues and summer months book out quickly - June, July, and August in particular.
May through September offers the best weather and longest daylight hours - golden hour in June doesn't begin until after 9pm, creating extraordinary light for photos. However, Ireland is stunning year-round. Off-season weddings (October through April) have their own magic - dramatic skies, fewer tourists, more venue availability, and often lower prices. We help you choose the right timing for your vision.
Yes - Ireland is one of the most accessible European destinations from the US. Direct transatlantic flights operate from New York, Boston, Chicago, Philadelphia, and other major cities to Dublin and Shannon airports. Ireland is English-speaking, the driving is straightforward (though on the left), and the country is compact enough to be explored easily. We include full guest travel guidance in your planning package.
