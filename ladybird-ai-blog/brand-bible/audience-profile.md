# LADYBIRD EVER AFTER — Audience Profile
## Who We Write For & What They Need

---

## The Core Audience

### Primary Persona: The American Couple Planning an Irish Wedding

**Demographics:**
- Age: 25–45 (average 30)
- Location: USA (all states, but concentrations in coastal cities, Atlanta, Austin, Denver)
- Household income: $75K–$200K+
- Relationship status: Engaged or seriously dating (planning 6–18 months out)
- First marriage: 80% first marriage; 15% second marriage, 5% vow renewal

**Psychographics:**
- Values: Adventure, authenticity, experience over stuff
- Mindset: "We're doing something unconventional and we're okay with that"
- Tech-savvy: Comfortable researching online, Instagram-native, digital communication
- Decision-making: Researches extensively before reaching out; wants clear information

**Pain Points (Unstated Fears):**
1. **Geography anxiety:** "How do we plan a wedding 4,000 miles away?"
2. **Complexity anxiety:** "Irish laws are different. What if we mess up the paperwork?"
3. **Vendor anxiety:** "How do we trust vendors we've never met?"
4. **Money anxiety:** "Will this cost a fortune? What about currency?"
5. **Guest anxiety:** "How do we ask our family to fly to Ireland?"
6. **Authenticity anxiety:** "Will it feel real? Will it feel like us?"

**Unspoken Questions:**
- Is this actually possible / legal / doable?
- How much is this going to cost?
- Do we need a planner, or can we DIY?
- What happens if something goes wrong?
- Will our family understand why we're doing this?

## Secondary Personas

### Persona 2: The Vow Renewal Couple
**Profile:**
- Age: 35–60+
- Married: 5+ years (often 15+)
- Mindset: "We want to celebrate us again, without all the wedding stress"
- Pain: Want to renew vows without the burden of a full wedding
- Ladder: Often budget-conscious; Fáilte package

**What they need:**
- Symbolic ceremony guidance (no legal remarriage)
- Small group coordination (usually immediate family)
- Simplicity and ease
- Ireland for the romance, not for the complexity

**Where to reach them:**
- Google: "vow renewal ireland"
- Instagram: "#vowrenewal"
- Content: Short, practical guides; real couple stories

## The Customer Journey & Content Needs

### **Stage 1: Awareness (Dreaming)**
*"Is getting married in Ireland even possible?"*

**What they're researching:**
- "Can Americans get married in Ireland?"
- "What does an Irish wedding cost?"
- "How long does it take to plan?"
- "Do we need a planner?"

**Content they need:**
- Blog posts: "Getting Married in Ireland as an American" (SEO anchor)
- Homepage: Clear, simple positioning ("Plan your Irish wedding from home")
- FAQs: Legal requirements, costs, timelines
- Instagram: Inspiration + real couple stories

**Tone needed:** Encouraging, demystifying, permission-giving

**Example headline:** "Yes, You Can Get Married in Ireland. Here's How."

---

### **Stage 2: Consideration (Researching)**
*"Who can help? What are my options? What would this actually cost?"*

**What they're researching:**
- Competitor websites (Eloping in Ireland, Peach Perfect, Twin Flames)
- Ladybird pricing and packages
- Real couple testimonials
- Venue options
- "What makes you different?"

**Content they need:**
- Packages page: Clear pricing, what's included, comparisons
- Venue pages: Beautiful photos + practical details
- Blog posts: Comparison posts, "how to choose a planner"
- "About us" page: Founder story (this is your biggest differentiator)
- Testimonials: Real quotes from real couples

**Tone needed:** Credible, confident, honest about what's unique

**Example headline:** "Why American Couples Choose Ladybird Ever After"

---

### **Stage 3: Decision (Getting in Touch)**
*"Okay, let's do this. What's next?"*

**What they're doing:**
- Filling out the contact form
- Emailing with questions
- Reading package details intensely
- Watching for social proof

**Content they need:**
- Clear CTA on every page ("Tell us about your vision")
- FAQ page: Common pre-contact questions
- Pricing page: All inclusions transparent, no surprises
- Welcome email: Founder story + next steps
- Sales page elements: Trust signals, testimonials, guarantees

**Tone needed:** Warm, welcoming, action-oriented

**Example headline:** "Tell Us About Your Vision. We'll Make It Real."

---

### **Stage 4: Booking (Post-Contact)**
*"Great, we're working with Ladybird. What happens now?"*

**What they need:**
- Clear next steps
- Regular communication from dedicated planner
- Practical guides (legal requirements, guest communication, vendor communication)
- Budget tracking
- Timeline management

**Content they need:**
- Welcome packet (email or PDF)
- Guides: "How to tell your family," "What to pack," "Irish customs"
- Regular check-in emails
- Vendor intros / brief documents
- Timeline dashboard

**Tone needed:** Supportive, organized, protective of their peace of mind

---

### **Stage 5: Post-Wedding (Advocacy)**
*"That was incredible! I want to tell everyone about Ladybird."*

**What they do:**
- Leave reviews (Google, WeddingWire, social)
- Tag Ladybird on Instagram
- Recommend to friends
- Potentially become a featured "happy couple" story

**Content they need:**
- Easy share buttons on confirmation emails
- Instagram photo packages to repost
- Referral program mechanics (optional)
- Permission to feature their story on the website

**Tone needed:** Gratitude, celebration, invitation to be part of the story

## Content Needs by Package Type

### **Fáilte Couples** (2–4 guests, $4,900)
**Who they are:** The intimate, minimalist couple. Solo elopement or very close family only.

**Pain points:**
- "Will it feel real with just 2 of us?"
- "Do we still need a planner?"
- "Can we keep this simple and affordable?"

**Content they respond to:**
- Solo/duo elopement stories (blog + Instagram)
- "Elopement vs. wedding" explainers
- Intimate venue spotlights
- Photo-focused stories (the couple was the priority, not the guest list)

**Messaging:** "Your wedding is yours alone. No compromise."

---

### **Grá Couples** (5–12 guests, $7,300)
**Who they are:** The adventurous couple with a small core group. Best friends + parents or immediate family.

**Pain points:**
- "How do we include people we love without a big wedding?"
- "Will it feel celebratory with a small group?"
- "How do we keep costs reasonable?"

**Content they respond to:**
- Elopement + dinner format stories
- "Bringing your tribe" guides
- Small-venue spotlights (castles, estates, pubs)
- Day-trip and adventure angle (combine wedding + trip)

**Messaging:** "Your people. Your pace. Pure celebration."

---

### **Sláinte Couples** (13–25 guests, $8,900)
**Who they are:** The sweet-spot couple. Intimate wedding with the feel of a full ceremony. Highest converter.

**Pain points:**
- "We want a real wedding, but small"
- "Will our family take it seriously?"
- "Can we have first dances, toasts, a celebration?"
- "How do we manage 15–20 people logistically?"

**Content they respond to:**
- Full wedding stories (even though guest count is small)
- Reception + dinner options
- "Intimate wedding ≠ less real" messaging
- Logistics guides (guest accommodations, welcome dinner, day-after activities)

**Messaging:** "All the magic of a full wedding. None of the overwhelm."

---

### **Ard Couples** (26–50 guests, $12,200)
**Who they are:** The traditional wedding couple, but doing it in Ireland. Wants full wedding experience.

**Pain points:**
- "Can we actually coordinate 30+ people internationally?"
- "How do we make this feel cohesive?"
- "Where does everyone stay?"
- "What about the reception?"

**Content they respond to:**
- Large-group stories (weddings with bands, speeches, room blocks)
- "Coordinating your guests from afar" guides
- Venue spotlights emphasizing reception capacity
- Multi-day itinerary ideas

**Messaging:** "Your full wedding day. Exceptional locations. Zero logistics stress."

## Messaging Priorities by Audience Segment

### **For First-Time Researchers**
- Emphasize: Legitimacy, legality, simplicity of the concept
- Lead with: Founder story + real couple examples
- Answer: "Is this actually possible?"

### **For Package Shoppers**
- Emphasize: All-in pricing, what's included, no surprise fees
- Lead with: Competitive advantages (USD, US-based, vendor management)
- Answer: "Why should I choose Ladybird?"

### **For Comparison Shoppers**
- Emphasize: Founder story, US-based entity, full vendor management
- Lead with: USD contract (no FX risk), dedicated planner, proof of experience
- Answer: "What makes you different from [competitor]?"

### **For Decision-Makers**
- Emphasize: Next steps, ease of process, support structure
- Lead with: Clear package details, CTA, testimonials
- Answer: "What happens after I contact you?"

## What They DON'T Want to Read

- ❌ Generic wedding industry advice (applies to any wedding, anywhere)
- ❌ Overly poetic descriptions of Ireland (they want practical + beautiful)
- ❌ Competitor comparisons (let their research speak for itself)
- ❌ Pressure tactics ("Limited availability!" "Book now!")
- ❌ Assumptions about their relationship or wedding vision
- ❌ Over-explanation of basic wedding concepts
- ❌ Cheesy couple language ("fairytale," "magical," "happily ever after")

## How to Write for Different Audience Segments

### **Writing for First-Time Researchers**
- Start with reassurance: "Yes, this is legitimate and legal"
- Explain the concept as though they've never heard it
- Use short sentences and clear structure
- Link to foundational pages (About, FAQs)
- Include real couple examples early

### **Writing for Package Shoppers**
- Lead with competitive advantages (USD, founder story, full management)
- Be specific about pricing and inclusions
- Compare directly if relevant ("Unlike [competitor], we...")
- Address common objections upfront
- Include social proof (testimonials, couple stories)

### **Writing for Decision-Makers**
- Focus on next steps and process clarity
- Emphasize support and relationship
- Use specific details (timeline, communication cadence)
- Include multiple CTAs
- Reduce friction to booking

### **Writing for Different Package Levels**
- **Fáilte:** Emphasize intimacy, simplicity, photography
- **Grá:** Emphasize adventure, small group, celebration
- **Sláinte:** Emphasize the full wedding experience, professional coordination
- **Ard:** Emphasize scale, logistics, full-day coverage

## The Golden Rule for Audience Writing

**Every piece of content should answer one of these unspoken questions:**
1. Is this possible?
2. Can I trust you?
3. What does this cost?
4. What happens next?
5. Will it feel real?

If it doesn't answer one of these, reframe the content before publishing.
