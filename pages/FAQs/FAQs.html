Elopements in Ireland

Understanding Elopements

New to elopements? Here's everything you need to know about this modern, intimate way to get married.

An elopement is a small, intimate wedding ceremony - typically around 16 guests or fewer. These are your nearest and dearest - the people you're closest to and already know what they're up to. You're not sending out formal invitations, RSVPs, or setting up a wedding website. You simply gather the people who matter most.

After the ceremony, the celebration is intentionally intimate - think a private room in a restaurant or a reserved dinner table rather than a DJ, first dance, and a party into the small hours. The focus is on the ceremony itself and a beautiful, relaxed celebration afterwards.

Modern elopements are nothing like the "running away to get married in secret" of the past. Today, they are intentional, beautifully planned celebrations of love - just without the guest list of 200 and the year of stressful planning.

Ireland is one of the most romantic and welcoming places in the world to elope. Here's why couples choose it:

  • No residency requirement: Unlike many countries, Ireland allows foreigners to legally marry without living there first. American and Canadian couples can have a fully legal wedding.
  • Breathtaking locations: From the Cliffs of Moher to ancient castle ruins to the wild Kerry coastline, Ireland offers some of the most dramatic wedding backdrops on earth.
  • Stress-free: With fewer details to manage, you can focus on what matters. We handle the planning, so your role is simple - make a few decisions and enjoy the experience.
  • Cost-effective: An Irish elopement typically costs a fraction of a large US wedding, freeing up your budget for a honeymoon, a home, or simply peace of mind.
  • It's entirely about you: Every detail is tailored to your love story - from the location to the vows to the celebratory pint of Guinness afterwards.

The legal process for getting married in the Republic of Ireland involves four main steps:

1. Notification (3+ months before): You submit a notification of your intention to marry to the HSE. This can be initiated remotely - you don't need to be in Ireland to start the process.

2. Documentation: You provide the required documents (passport, birth certificate, etc.). We review everything before you submit.

3. Acknowledgement: The HSE issues a Marriage Registration Form once your notification is approved. This typically takes a few weeks.

4. Ceremony: Your registered celebrant performs the ceremony. After signing the Marriage Registration Form, your marriage is legally binding under Irish law and recognised internationally, including in the United States.

Getting married in Northern Ireland? The process is different - you need to notify the local registrar between 14 and 28 days before your ceremony. However, we recommend doing this as early as possible to allow time to collect your paperwork in person.

We handle the coordination of this entire process for both jurisdictions. You'll never have to navigate Irish bureaucracy alone.

The legal minimum age to get married is 18 years old in both the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland. There are no exceptions and no parental consent provisions for younger marriages in either jurisdiction. Both parties must be at least 18 on the date of the ceremony.

Travel & Logistics

Getting there & getting ready

Practical details for travelling from the US to Ireland for your wedding.

Ireland is beautiful year-round, and we plan elopements in every season. Each offers something different:

May – September (peak season): The longest daylight hours (sunset as late as 10pm in June!), milder temperatures (15–20°C / 60–68°F), and lush green landscapes. This is the most popular season, so book early.

October – November (autumn): Golden light, dramatic skies, stunning foliage, and far fewer tourists at popular locations. Slightly cooler but still beautiful.

December – February (winter): Moody, atmospheric, and deeply romantic. Shorter days but magical light. Castle venues with roaring fires create an incredible backdrop.

March – April (spring): Wildflowers, baby lambs in the fields, and increasing daylight. A lovely time for photos.
