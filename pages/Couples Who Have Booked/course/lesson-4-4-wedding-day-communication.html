When Ladybird Has a Coordinator

When a Ladybird team member is on the ground at your venue, they run the day. Your job is to get dressed, enjoy the morning, and show up where you are supposed to be. We handle vendor arrivals, timeline management, troubleshooting, and communication between all parties. You do not need to manage anything.

When You're Running Your Own Day

For some packages and arrangements, couples manage the wedding day timeline themselves. If this applies to you, here is what you will have and what you will need to do.

Your Day-Of Document

You will be given a comprehensive Day-Of Document before your wedding. This document contains your complete minute-by-minute timeline, every vendor's name and contact number, the full venue address and any access instructions, and all the logistical details your wedding party and guests need to know.

Before your wedding day, we work with you to review this document together. This gives you the chance to add comments, clarify timing, note any concerns, and ensure there's no ambiguity about the day's logistics. We want you to feel completely prepared and confident in what's happening, minute by minute. Read it. Know it. Share the relevant sections with whoever is helping you on the day.

WhatsApp Is Your Communication Tool

All of your Irish vendors communicate via WhatsApp. Before you land in Ireland, make sure you have WhatsApp downloaded and that you have activated either an international data plan or an eSIM (see Lesson 3.2). You will be added to a WhatsApp group that includes you, your partner, and all of your suppliers. This is the group where any day-of communication happens-a vendor running slightly behind, a question about timing, a change to the plan. Keep notifications on.

Your vendor group chat is your direct line to everyone involved in your day. If something comes up, message the group. Vendors in Ireland are responsive and professional, and clear communication through the group keeps everyone aligned.

The golden rule: Read your Day-Of Document the evening before your wedding. Know your timeline. Know your vendors' numbers. Know who is arriving when and where. The couples who feel calmest on their wedding morning are always the ones who did this.
