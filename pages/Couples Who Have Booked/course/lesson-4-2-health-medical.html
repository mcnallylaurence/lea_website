Ireland's Medical Facilities

Ireland is a safe, well-developed country with good medical facilities. You are extremely unlikely to need emergency care during your trip. That said, a few things are worth knowing before you land.

Your US health insurance almost certainly does not cover medical treatment abroad. If you need to see a doctor or visit an emergency room in Ireland, you will typically be required to pay out of pocket and submit a claim for reimbursement when you return home-if your plan covers international care at all. This is another reason travel insurance with medical coverage matters.

Irish Pharmacies

For minor issues-a cold, a headache, an upset stomach, a small cut-Irish pharmacies are well-stocked and the pharmacists are genuinely knowledgeable and helpful. In Ireland, pharmacists can advise on and dispense a wider range of medications without a prescription than is typical in the US. If you feel unwell before or during your trip, a pharmacy is often the fastest and most practical first stop.

Look for the green cross sign - that is the universal pharmacy symbol in Ireland. Most towns of any size have at least one.

Prescription Medications

If you take prescription medication regularly, bring more than you think you need. Getting a US prescription filled in Ireland is complicated and sometimes impossible. Carry your medications in their original labeled packaging and keep them in your carry-on bag, never in checked luggage.
