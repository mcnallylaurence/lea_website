Contracts, Invoicing & VAT

One agreement, kept simple

To keep things straightforward for couples who are often travelling from the US, they sign one master agreement with us. Here is what that means for you.

The Agreement

You enter into a contract with Ladybird Events Limited, our Irish-based company. We sign your agreement on behalf of the couple, so there is no need for you to manage a separate client contract.

VAT on Invoices

Because this is a service provided within Ireland between two Irish businesses, VAT must be included on invoices where applicable. We understand that some celebrants do not charge VAT on religious services, and we are happy to work with whatever applies to your practice.

Payment Timeline

Our preference is to pay a deposit on booking confirmation and settle the balance in the week before the ceremony. Everyone runs their business differently though, and we will always do our best to meet your terms. Share your payment schedule with us and we will work together to make it fit.

Insurance

We require all partners to maintain current Public Liability Insurance. Please ensure your cover is up to date before any booking is confirmed.

Cancellations

If a couple cancels, your agreed-upon cancellation policy stands. We will always communicate any changes to a booking as early as possible.

Travel

We try wherever possible to work with celebrants who are local to the area, so that you know the landscape and no travel costs need to be factored in. If a booking does require travel on your part, please flag this when we send you the initial enquiry so we can include it in the quote from the start.

After Booking is Confirmed

Meeting your couple for the first time

Once your booking is confirmed and your deposit has been paid, we hand things over to you. Here is exactly how that works and what we ask of you in those first few days.

1

You Receive the Day-Of Document

As soon as the booking is confirmed and your deposit is with you, we send you our Day-Of Document. This is a Google Doc that lives with the wedding from confirmation all the way through to the day itself. It holds everything we know at that point: the couple's names, their email address, the date, the locations, and any detail that is already locked in. The document is a living one - we update it continuously as the timeline is built out and new information comes in. You will always have access to the most current picture of the day.

2

Add Them to Your Own System

The Day-Of Document gives you everything you need to add the couple to your own CRM or client management system. Their names and email address are yours to use as you would with any couple who had come to you directly. Please treat this information with the same care and discretion you would extend to any client.

3

Send Your First Email - With Us on CC

Your very first email to the couple should include us on CC at info@ladybirdeverafter.com. That is all we need. Reach out exactly as you would if they had come to you directly - introduce yourself, let them know you are so excited to be holding the space for their ceremony, and take it from there in whatever way feels natural to you. There is no script from us. This is your relationship to build.

4

After That, It Is All Yours

Once we have seen that first email land, you do not need to copy us in on anything else. All your ongoing correspondence with the couple - the questionnaires, the ceremony consultation calls, the script drafts, the legal paperwork - is entirely between you and them. We do not need to be in the loop on every message. We just want to know you made that first connection.

A note on the Day-Of Document: Because the Google Doc is updated continuously as we plan the day together, it is always worth checking back in the weeks before the ceremony. Timeline slots, meeting points, location confirmations, and guest counts are added progressively. If you ever have a question about something that is not in there yet, just ask - we will either have the answer or we will find it.

Working with the Couple

Getting to know them before the day

The ceremony is the heart of everything. The more you know about this couple before you step into that space together, the better the day will be. We ask that you give this relationship the same care and time you would give any couple you work with independently.

The Getting-to-Know-You Call

We require at least one video call or deep-dive session before the wedding day. Most of our couples are travelling from the US and this call is often the first time they hear your voice. We want them to feel like they are meeting a friend when you arrive at the ceremony, not a stranger holding a script.

Understanding Their Story

Beyond the call, take the time to understand who they are. Their relationship, how they met, what matters to them, what they want the ceremony to feel like. The couples who book Ladybird tend to be thoughtful, romantic people. They will notice if a ceremony feels generic, and they will never forget one that feels completely theirs.

Rehearsals

For most of our elopements, a formal rehearsal is not required or expected. If you feel a rehearsal would benefit the couple, particularly for more complex ceremonies or locations, discuss it with us and we will factor it into the planning. If a rehearsal is needed, we will make sure it is accounted for in your fee.

Ceremony & Script

Their words, your craft

The script is yours to lead. We trust your experience and your instinct completely. What we ask is that the couple feels heard in it, and that the ceremony reflects who they actually are rather than a template of who couples are supposed to be.

Script Approval

We ask that the couple has the opportunity to review and approve the ceremony script before the day. How you manage that process is up to you - you know your workflow. We simply ask that no couple arrives at their ceremony hearing words for the first time that do not feel like them.

Vows

Many of our couples choose to write their own vows. If they do, please give them clear guidance on length, tone, and how the exchange will work on the day. Couples who are nervous benefit enormously from knowing exactly what to expect.

Ceremony Length

Most of our elopement ceremonies run between 20 and 40 minutes. Please be clear with the couple about what fits within their package and flag anything to us if the scope starts to grow beyond what was agreed.

Readings & Rituals

If the couple wants to include readings, unity rituals, or other personal elements, work these into the ceremony and the timeline. Let us know if anything requires additional setup or props so we can include it in the day's planning.

Legal Requirements

You are the expert. You lead this.

When it comes to everything legal, you are the authority in the room. We are planners, not solemnisers, and we will never pretend otherwise. We rely completely on your expertise to ensure that every legally binding ceremony we plan is executed correctly, compliantly, and with confidence. This section outlines everything we expect you to own and lead on our behalf and on behalf of the couple.

◆
The legal portion of every ceremony is entirely your domain. We ask that you approach it with the same expertise, proactivity, and confidence that you bring to the ceremony itself. If something is not right, we need to know early. If you are unsure about anything, raise it with us and we will work through it together. But the knowledge, the preparation, and the leadership on all things legal sit with you.

Solemniser Registration

For any legally binding ceremony in the Republic of Ireland or Northern Ireland, you must be a registered solemniser in the correct jurisdiction. Please ensure your registration is current and valid before accepting any legally binding booking with us. If your registration is due for renewal, flag it to us at the point of enquiry - never after a booking is confirmed.

Three-Month Notification

Couples getting legally married in Ireland must give three months' notice to their local civil registration office. This is the couple's legal responsibility, but it is yours to advise on and ours to flag at booking. Please confirm with the couple directly that this has been done, and do so well in advance of the day - not in the week before. If it has not been filed, that needs to be known immediately.

Witnesses

A legal ceremony in Ireland requires two witnesses who are 18 or older. It is your responsibility to advise the couple on this requirement clearly and early. If a couple is eloping with no guests and has not arranged witnesses, we need to know in advance so we can work together to source them. Please do not leave witness logistics to the day itself - it is too important and too easy to solve ahead of time.

Witness Information & Signatures

You are responsible for collecting the required information from witnesses - including full names and dates of birth - and ensuring the correct legal documentation is completed and signed on the day. Please be proactive about obtaining this in advance where possible, and bring everything you need to the ceremony location so nothing is left to chance.

HSE & Civil Registration Filing

Following the ceremony, the legal documentation must be filed correctly with the HSE or the relevant civil registration authority, depending on the jurisdiction. This is your responsibility as the solemniser. If you have any questions about filing requirements or timelines, please resolve them before the wedding day, not after. The couple is counting on the legal marriage being registered correctly and promptly.

Northern Ireland Differences

If a booking is in Northern Ireland, the legal framework is different from the Republic. Registration is handled through the General Register Office for Northern Ireland, notice requirements differ, and the solemniser requirements are separate. Please ensure you are registered and familiar with the correct process for the jurisdiction of each booking. We will always tell you which side of the border a ceremony falls on.

Location Legality - Outdoor & Protected Sites

Not all ceremony locations can legally host a binding marriage. Many of our most beloved outdoor locations - clifftops, beaches, mountain passes, forest clearings - are not licensed for legal ceremonies in the Republic of Ireland. You are responsible for knowing this and advising us and the couple accordingly. If a couple has their heart set on a location where a legal ceremony cannot take place, we need your guidance on the options: whether that means a symbolic ceremony at the dream location, a legal signing at a nearby hotel or registry office, or a combination of both.

The Backup Legal Location

For couples planning outdoor or non-licensed ceremonies who want a legally binding marriage, we expect you to come to the table with a recommendation for a backup legal location. This might be a hotel you have an existing relationship with, a local venue that is licensed, or a civil registration office. Do not wait for us to solve this - you know the local landscape far better than we do, and having a trusted backup location in your back pocket is part of what makes you invaluable to our couples.

Local Knowledge as a Superpower

Many of our celebrants have strong relationships with local hotels, venues, and civil registration offices built up over years of working in a region. We actively encourage you to use those relationships. If a hotel you know can host a quick legal signing before a couple heads to the Cliffs for their ceremony, that is a beautiful solution and one we want to know about. Share your local knowledge with us freely - it makes the whole experience better for everyone.

Advising Us as Well as the Couple

When you identify a legal consideration that affects the day - whether that is a location constraint, a documentation gap, or a question about witness availability - please advise both the couple and Ladybird at the same time. We need to be aware of anything that could affect the planning or the timeline. You are never dealing with this alone, but you are always the one who leads it.

A note on symbolic ceremonies: Not every couple who books Ladybird wants or needs a legally binding ceremony. Some are already legally married at home and are choosing Ireland purely for the experience, the landscape, and the meaning of the moment. For these couples, none of the legal requirements above apply. We will always be clear with you about which kind of ceremony each booking involves. If you are ever unsure, ask us before you reach out to the couple.

On the Day

Your role is to hold the space, not manage it

We take the logistics completely off your plate. Every Ladybird day comes with a detailed timeline, a clear meeting point, and a full brief so you know exactly what is expected before you arrive. Your job is to be present, grounded, and focused on the couple.

We Provide the Timeline

Before the wedding day, you will receive a Ladybird timeline that covers where to be, when to be there, and how the day flows. We build this collaboratively in the weeks leading up and share it with everyone on the team so nothing falls through the cracks.

Transportation Is Not Your Responsibility

We never expect you to transport a couple. Couples either hire professional transportation, drive themselves, or travel with family members. If you ever choose to offer a lift as a personal gesture, that is entirely at your own risk and outside the scope of your agreement with us.

On the Day Coordination

Some of our larger packages include on the day coordination. For couples who opt out of that, we do not expect you to step into that role. You follow the Ladybird timeline and we all work as a team. Celebrants naturally become an anchor on the day, but shepherding the schedule is not something we ask of you unless it has been specifically agreed and compensated.

We Are Always Reachable

We are not a big corporation. There is no ticketing system or support queue. If you have a question, a concern, or just need to check something before the day, WhatsApp us, send an email, or give us a call. We are a small team and we genuinely mean it when we say our door is always open.

Crediting Your Work

We credit you wherever we can. On Instagram we tag you, on our website we tag you, and we do the same at every touchpoint. Occasionally a tag gets missed. It is never intentional. If you spot something that has not been credited correctly, just let us know and we will fix it straight away.

When We Work

A lot of our ceremonies happen midweek, which opens up the calendar in a way that works for everyone. We do weekend days too. Whatever the day, we will have everything prepared and ready for you well in advance. We are grateful for every gap in your calendar that you share with us.

The What Ifs

Answers for the unexpected

We have thought through the edge cases so you do not have to worry about them. Here is how we handle things if plans change.

Your agreed-upon cancellation policy stands in full. We do not override your terms. If a couple cancels, we will notify you promptly and any deposits or fees already paid to you are governed by the terms in your agreement with us.
We understand that life happens. If you cannot make it due to an emergency, we expect you to lead the search for a trusted, high-quality replacement. If no replacement can be found, a full refund of all monies paid is required so the couple can secure a last-minute alternative. We are happy to assist in the search where we can.
We build buffer time into every Ladybird timeline. That said, if you feel the ceremony is growing in scope before the day, flag it with us early. If the day itself runs a little long, use your judgement and let us know afterwards. We will never leave you in an awkward position because of something that was beyond your control.
If something arises that affects the legal standing of the ceremony, please handle it with care and professionalism and contact us as soon as possible. For legally binding ceremonies, any issue with documentation, missing witness information, or registration should be flagged to us immediately so we can support the couple and work through the best path forward together.
Witness logistics should always be confirmed well before the wedding day. If a couple has no guests and no witnesses arranged, this needs to be flagged to us as early as possible so we can work together to find a solution in advance. If something unexpected happens on the day, contact us immediately and we will do everything we can to help. Do not attempt to improvise with unvetted individuals without consulting us first.
Script changes are part of the process and we encourage couples to feel ownership of their ceremony right up to the day. If changes become so significant that they affect your preparation time or the integrity of the ceremony, have an honest conversation with the couple and loop us in. We are always here to help navigate those moments.
